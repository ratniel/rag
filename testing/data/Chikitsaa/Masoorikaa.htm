Masoorikaa Chikitsaa

 

Reference:

C.Chi.12/92-93, S. Chi. 20/31, B.R.59, Y.R. Uttaraardha Masoorikaa Chikitsaa, N.R. Masoorikaa

 

Introduction:

Masoorikaa is a disease in which pustules spread all over the body due to vitiated Pitta and Kapha Dosha. It is classified under the Kshudraroga category in classical texts; later due to its recurrence and severity it has become noteworthy.

Similarly Masoorikaa is also one of the Prameha Peed`akaa_s. It is different from the earlier mentioned disease.

 

Precautions

  • In Jvara (fever) the patient should be kept away from water and air.
  • Vijayaa powder should be sprinkled over the body and body should be wrapped in clean and soft cloth.

 

Saamaanya Chikitsaa

 

 

 

 

  • S`hodhana should be administered after Langhana.
  • In Aahaara vegetables as well as meat (Pratuda Maamsa) both are advised.
  • Amritaadi Kashaaya from Visarpa Chikitsaa should be used.

 

 

 

Vis`hesha Chikitsaa

 

Vaataja Masoorikaa Medication should be started with Laajaa Tarpan`a.

  • Paadadaaha Tan`d`ulaambu Seka should be given
  • Paakakaala Brimhan`a should be given to prevent Vaata Prakopa, as in this stage Vaata is already induces S`hoshan`a of pustules.

 

Aavasthika Chikitsaa

 

  • Upadrava Chikitsaa

 

    • S`hoola + Aadhmaana + Kampa: Dhanva Maamsarasa + Saindhava

 

    • Khadira + Bahuvaara Siddha Jala should be used for S`hauchakaarya. (Hasta Prakshaalana, Guda Prakshaalana)

 

    • Vran`a: Vran`a Chikitsaa should be done.

 

    • Dusht`a Vran`a: Jalaukaavacharan`a + Vran`as`hothahara Yoga.

 

    • Mukharoga + Kan`t`haroga: Gan`d`oosha

 

    • Netragata Masoorikaa: Netra Seka by S`hambookodara Kosha Neera (water collected from inside the shell), Karpura Choorn`a etc

 

    • Sraava Avachoorn`ana, Dhoopana
