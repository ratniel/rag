∑        The indicated medicine or unctuous substance administered through nose is Nasya or errhines.

 

Description:

∑        As preventive measure Nasya should be instilled in spring season by Teekshn`a (intense) drugs for a healthy person.

 

Poorvakarma:

∑        Nasya should be administered inside a room. This room should be free from direct and excessive wind flow and dust.

∑        Instrument checklist:

1.    Nasya Aasana.

2.    Nasya Aushadhi according to the sub type.

3.    Nasya Yantra essential only for Dhmaapana type of Nasya.

4.    Sht`heevana Paatra.

5.    Cloth for cleansing.

6.    Towel for physician.

∑        Patient preparation:

Prior to procedure:

o       Patient should be asked to evacuate bowel and pass urine.

o       Proper diet should be given.

o       Danta Dhaavana (brushing teeth) should be done.

o       Adequate Dhoomapaana (medicated smoking) should be administered.

o       Patient should lie down on the Nasya S`hayyaa (figure).

o       Oil massage should be applied over scalp.

o       Mild fomentation should be applied over head.

o       Taapa Sveda should be administered above the clavicular region. I.e. head, face, nose, neck (Manyaa, Greevaa, Kant`ha).

o       This should be followed by smooth massage over following regions i.e. Gala (neck), Kapola (cheeks), Lalaat`a Pradesha (forehead).

∑        Medicine preparation:

o       Medicine according to the subtypes.

 

Pradhaanakarma:

1.    Patient should lie down in a supine position. Head should not be excessively flexed or extended.

2.    The position should be slightly head low.

3.    The patient should avoid speech, anger & laughing during the procedure.

4.    Fomentation and massage above the clavicular region should be applied.

5.    After this the suitable medicine should be administered by physician in an adequate dose by following method. The medicine should be made warm by keeping inside a hot water bath. This warm medicine should be administered. The physician should elevate the nose of patient by his left thumb and then administer medicine with right hand to each nostril.

6.    The patient should avoid swallowing the medicine.

7.    The patient is asked to lie down for a time required for counting up to 100 numbers. The patient should be advised to turn on one side and slowly get up without jerking.

8.    Patient should eliminate Kapha by Sht`heevana (spitting it out).

Ch.Si.9 / 104, S.Chi.40 / 26-27, A.H.Su.20 / 20-22

 

Pas`hchaatkarma:

1.    Dhoomapaana Medicated smoking.

2.    Kavala Graha and Gan`d`oosha Dhaaran`a Gargles or withholding the suitable medicine or warm water in the mouth.

3.    Diet Warm water for drinking.

4.    Light diet.

5.    Following things should be avoided immediately after the procedure

o       Cold water contact.

o       Abhishyandi Aahaara (The diet which is capable of causing secretions of various fluids and thus resulting in occlusion of various environments or channels).

o       Rajodhooma (dust and fumes)

o       Sneha (Contact or intake of unctuous drugs)

o       Aatapa (Sun rays)

o       Madya (alcohol)

o       Head bath

o       Atiyaana (traveling on a fast vehicle)

o       Krodha (anger)

o       Nidraa (sleep)

Ch.Si.9 / 106, S.Chi.40 / 31
