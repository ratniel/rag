Chikitsaa Prakaara: Snehana (Oleation)

 

Prakaara Paribhaashaa:

        Snehanam Sneha Vishyanda Maardava Kleda Kaarakam.

        The procedure in which moistening, breaking away of the bonds between Dosha and Dhaatu, and liquefaction is achieved with the help of external and internal application of unctuous medicines is termed as Snehana.

        Snehana is a principle function of Jala Mahaabhoota the attribute which is Snigdha.

 

Types:

        Main two types of internal oleation:

1.    S`hodhana Sneha

2.    S`hamana Sneha

        Three types of internal oleation

1.    Achchha Snehapaana only the unctuous medicine is consumed.

2.    Sneha Vichaaran`aa - unctuous medicine is consumed mixing with some food items.

3.    Sadya Sneha these are the medications which result in immediate oleation.

 

Description:Kaala/Vaya/Ahoraatra/Ritu/Bhukta

        For S`hodhana type of oleation

o       The medicine should be administered after the previous meal is completely digested.

o       Oleation should be administered on a sunny day and during moderate seasonal conditions.

o       For Kapha dominance or Vaatakapha dominance and during cold seasons Oleation should be administered during day time.

o       For Vaatapitta dominance and during hot seasons Oleation should be administered during night.

o       For internal oleation as a pre-procedure of Panchakarma the medicine should be administered in the morning at sunrise.

o       Ghrita should be used for oleation during S`harad Ritu, Vasaa or Majjaa should be used during Vais`haakha and Taila should be used during Praavrit` Ritu.

        For S`hamana type of oleation.

The medicine should be administered at the Kshutkaala (when one feels hungry).

 

Poorvakarma (Prerequisites):

        The dose and the appropriate medicine should be decided considering the Kosht`ha, Vyaadhibala and Rugn`abala.

Patient Preparation:

        Diet having following properties should be consumed by the patient on the previous day of oleation:

o       Liquid

o       Hot

o       Pramaan`ayukta (one should eat in adequate quantity not in excess).

o       Anabhishyandi (not too salty or sour which causes excess secretion of Kapha and Pitta which leads to occlusion of channels).

o       Na Sankeern`a (simple diet without mixing too many varieties).

o       Na Ati Snigdha (it should not be very greasy or oily).

 

Pradhaanakarma:

        For S`hodhana type of oleation:

1.    Svastivaachana.

2.    Patients counselling. Large amounts of unctuous substances make counselling mandatory.

3.    The patient may get feeling of nausea due to smell and colour of the medicine. To avoid this patients eyes should be closed with a cloth and then he should be advised to consume the medicine.

4.    To overcome nauseating feeling during consumption the medicine should be consumed along with lemon juice.

5.    Man`d`a, Yoosha or hot water can be used instead of lemon juice.

6.    If ghee is to be consumed it should be made lukewarm before consuming.

7.    The oleation medicine should be decided according to the Doshapraadhaanya in following manner

 

Dosha

Medicine

Vaata Praadhaanya

Ghrita + Lavan`a

Pitta Praadhaanya

Ghrita

Kapha Praadhaanya

Ghrita + Trikat`u + Kshaara

 

8.    If oleation is to be administered as a pre-procedure of Panchakarma then it should be administered for three / five / seven days according to the Kosht`ha. Due to this oleation of all Dhaatu will be achieved.

9.    After seven days the body becomes homologous to the oleation (Saatmya) and it doesnt produce the desired effect as a pre-procedure of Panchakarma.

10.                 Oleation should be stopped if signs of adequate Snehana appear.

11.                 The dose should be started with the Hraseeyasi Maatraa (2 and Tolaa).

12.                 The dose should be increased gradually each day according to the Agni, Kosht`ha, Vyaadhibala and Rugn`abala till the signs of adequate oleation appear.

13.                 Dose Decision The dose of the drug for oleation should be decided considering the Kosht`ha, Vyaadhibala, Rugn`a, Vyaadhi, Agni etc. The Uttama Maatraa of oleation is the one which takes 24 hours for digestion of the medicine. The Madhyama Maatraa is the one which takes 12 hours for digestion and Hrasva Maatraa is the one which takes 6 hours for digestion.

o       Uttama Sneha Maatraa is indicated in following Rugn`a Avasthaa or Vyaadhi The patient who is habituated to more intake of Sneha, to tolerate thirst and hunger, having good Agnibala (digestive power), having good strength, in Gulma, Sarpa Dasht`a, Visarpa, Unmaada, Mootrakrichchhra, Gaad`ha Varchas.

o       Madhyama Sneha Maatraa is indicated in following Rugn`a Avasthaa or Vyaadhi The patient having medium strength, Mridu Kost`ha, habituated to eating small amount of food, in Arushka, Sphot`a, Pid`ikaa, Kan`d`oo, Paamaa, Kusht`ha, Prameha, Vaatarakta.

o       Hrasva Sneha Maatraa is indicated in following Rugn`a Avasthaa or Vyaadhi The patient having poor strength, having poor Agnibala (digestive power), who cannot tolerate Riktakosht`ha, Vriddha, Baala, Sukumaara, Sukhochita, in Jvara, Atisaara, Kaasa.

14.                 Following signs appear when the Sneha is getting digested

o       S`hirorujaa (headache)

o       Bhrama (giddiness)

o       Laalaasraava (increased salivation)

o       Moorchchhaa (fainting)

o       Saada (tiredness)

o       Klama (fatigue)

o       Trishn`aa (increased thirst)

o       Daaha (personal sensation of burning)

o       Arati (restlessness)

15.                 Following signs appear when the Sneha gets completely digested.

o       Disappearance of headache, etc

o       Laaghava (lightness of body, sense organs and mind)

o       Vaataanulomana (normal movement of Vaata along its own channels)

o       Kshudhaa Pravritti (induction of appetite).

o       Trishn`aa (thirst).

o       Udgaara S`huddhi (clear belching without smell, taste etc).

16.                 External oleation should also be administered along with internal oleation.

 

        For S`hamana type of oleation:

1.    Achchha type of oleation should be administered (single unctuous substance without any additives).

2.    Siddha Sneha should be consumed twice or thrice during day.

3.    If the oleation is to be administered for Brimhan`a (strengthening) then it should be administered as Sneha Vichaaran`aa and in a dose less than Hraseeyasi Maatraa daily.

It should be continued for a longer period than 7 days.

 

Pas`hchaatkarma:

        For S`hodhana type of oleation.

  1. The patient should be given hot water to drink throughout the day.
  2. Hot water bath.
  3. He should be advised to drink Yavaagu, Yoosha and Vilepi.
  4. He should be advised rest but not sleeping during the day.
  5. Brahmacharya should be adopted.
  6. Following things should be avoided immediately after the procedure.

o       Vegaavarodha (withholding natural urges)

o       Uchcha Bhaashan`a (talking high pitch persistently).

o       Krodha (anger)

o       S`hoka (grief)

o       Contact with too much hot or cold

o       Roaming in open air

o       Physical exertion

o       Travelling on a fast moving vehicle

o       Very high or low pillow while sleeping

o       Contact with dust

o       Medicated smoking

o       Excessive sleeping

o       Keeping awake late during night

o       Abhishyandi (causing channel blockage) and dry diet

  1. Vamana should be administered after a one day rest. External oleation should be administered on the rest day.
  2. Virechana should be administered after three days rest. External oleation should be administered on the rest days.

 

        For S`hamana type of oleation.

Laghu diet should be administered 1 and hours after consuming Sneha.
